The Process to Adopt a Child

1

Research Adoption Options

Decide on the type of adoption (domestic, international, private, foster care). Choose an agency or authorized organization that fits your preferences.

2

Meet Eligibility Requirements

Meet the age, marital status, financial, and health criteria set by the agency or country laws. Complete criminal and background checks.

3

Submit an Application

Provide detailed information about your family, lifestyle, and motivation for adoption. Pay application fees (if applicable).

4

Home Study

Undergo interviews with a social worker. Provide financial and health records, as well as references. Ensure your home environment is suitable for a child.

5

Complete Training/Education

Attend training sessions to prepare for the challenges of parenting adopted children.

6

Child Referral and Matching

Review child profiles to find a match. In foster-to-adopt or international cases, meet the child during a visitation period.

7

Adoption Finalization

Complete all legal paperwork. Attend court hearings to gain legal custody of the child. Receive a final adoption decree.

8

Post-Adoption Process

Participate in follow-up visits as required by law or agency. Access post-adoption counseling or resources to ease the transition for your family.
